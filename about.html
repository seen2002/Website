Rome

'Roma' believed to have been named after the city's founder and first king Romulus, was founded in 753 BC. The city started of as a tiny settlement of Latins by the river Tiber and grew from pastoral settlements on the palatine hill built above the area of the future Roman Forum. With a population of roughly 2.873 million as of 2017 spanning across 1,285km²/496.1sq miles, accompanied by the city's rich history and being the oldest continuously occupied city in Europe makes it very interesting.
Being one of the major cities in the Renaissance(A point in time between the 14th and 17th century that promoted the rediscovery of classical philosophy, literature, and art), with the likes of Leonardo Da Vinci, Michelangelo di Lodovico Buonarroti Simoni, Raffaello Sanzio da Urbino, and Donato di Niccolò di Betto Barbi. Which led it its historical centre being dubbed a world heritage site by UNESCO.


Did you know?

•Nearly €700,000 worth of coins are tossed into Rome's Trevi Fountain every year and all the proceeds go to the Caritas to help those in need.

•Laws in Rome allow cats to live without disruption in the place they were born. Meaning wild cats can climb the walls of the Colosseum and sleep among the Ruins of the Forum.

•The symbol SPQR which is found all over the city of Rome is an abbreviation of Senatus Populusque Romanus, which is translated to The Senate and The People of Rome. In reference to when the common folk(The Republic) fought and advocated for equal rights between them and the Senate(patres).




•The Museo Nazionale delle Paste Alimentari located in Rome is an entire museum dedicated to the history, evolution, production, and consumption of pasta. Which was established in 1993 with the help of the Vincenze Agnesi Foundation.

•Rome has the world's smallest sovereign state located in its city limits, the Vatican City. Which is also the home for the St. Peter's Basillica, the largest church ever constructed. St. Peter's Basillica also currently displaysthe chains that are said to have held St. Pietro while imprisoned in the Carcere Mamertion.

•Modern day rome is said to have 280 fountains and more than 900 churches and his home to Rome's first University. The La Sapienza, established in 1303 AD, is the largest University in europe and the 2nd largest in the world.
